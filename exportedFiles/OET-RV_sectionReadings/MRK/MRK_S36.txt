Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 7 24 to 30.
The section heading is: The bold faith of a non-Jewish woman.


Yeshua left that place and went up north into the regions of Tyre and Tsidon. He tried to enter a house without being noticed but didn't succeed, and straight away a woman who had heard about him turned up. She knelt in front of him because she had a little daughter who was demon-possessed. The woman was Greek from Phoenicia in Syria and she begged Yeshua to command the demon to leave her daughter. But he replied, “Let the Jewish children be treated first, because it's not good to take the children's food and throw it to the dogs.”

And she responded, “Yes, master, but the puppies under the table do get to eat the little crumbs that the children drop.”

And he answered, “Go home now. As a result of how you just spoke, the demon has already left your daughter.”

And when she got home, the demon had indeed gone and her little girl was lying quietly on her bed.
