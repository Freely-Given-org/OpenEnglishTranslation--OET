Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 8 1 to 10.
The section heading is: Yeshua feeds 4,000 people.


Another time there was a large crowd again with nothing to eat. Yeshua called his apprentices and told them, “I feel sorry for all these people because they've already been with us for three days and don't have anything to eat. I can't send them home because they're too weak to walk, and some have a long way to go.”

His apprentices queried, “Where could anyone find enough food out here in the wilderness?”

He asked, “How much bread have you all got?”

“Seven buns,” they answered.

So Yeshua instructed the crowd to sit down on the ground. Then he took the seven buns, gave thanks and broke them and gave pieces to his apprentices to then distribute to the crowd. They also had a few small cooked fish, so he blessed them too and got them to distribute them as well. After all the people had eaten and were full, they collected seven basketfuls of leftovers. There were some four thousand people there that day, and Yeshua sent them home after they'd eaten. And he and his apprentices climbed into the boat and headed for the Dalmanutha region.
