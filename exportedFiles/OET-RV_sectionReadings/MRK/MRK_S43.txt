Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 8 31 to 9:1.
The section heading is: Yeshua’s radical challenge to his would-be followers.


Then Yeshua began teaching his followers that humanity's child would need to suffer a lot, to be rejected by the religious leaders, and so to be put to death but to come back to life after three days. He taught this very clearly, but Peter took him aside and told him off. Yeshua turned back and looked at the others, then reprimanded Peter, “Stand back, Satan! you're applying human values instead of considering God's plan.”

Calling out to them and to the crowd, he said, “Anyone who wants to follow me must first put aside their own desires and then follow me—even through very difficult times. Anyone who worries about saving their own life will lose it, but anyone who gives their life due to following me and my teaching will save their life. Because it doesn't benefit a person if they gain everything in the world but lose their soul. What can someone offer in order to buy a home for their soul? Yes, anyone who is ashamed of me and my message in front of this adulterous and sinful generation, humanity's child will likewise be ashamed of them when he comes clearly displaying God's greatness and bringing the holy messengers.”

And he continued, “I can assure you that some of the ones standing here won't die until they have seen God's powerful kingdom arrive.”
