Next we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 15 33 to 41.
The section heading is: Yeshua breathes his last.


At noon, it got dark everywhere, until around 3pm, when Yeshua shouted loudly, “My god, my god, why have you abandoned me?”

Some of the people standing nearby thought he was calling the prophet Eliyah . One of them ran, and soaked a sponge in some sour wine, then put it on a stick and held it up for Yeshua to drink, saying, “Hang on. Let's see if Eliyah does come to get him down.”

But just then Yeshua uttered a loud cry and exhaled his last breath.

(Back in the temple, the curtain tore down the middle from the top to the bottom.) When Yeshua died, the Roman centurion who had been standing back watching everything said, “This man was definitely God's son.”

Further back, there was also a group of women observing everything. These included Maria from Magdala, Maria the mother of young Yacob and Yoses, and Salome, and others who had followed Yeshua around Galilee and helped look after him, along with many others who had come down with him to Yerushalem.
