Next we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 11 1 to 11.
The section heading is: Yeshua is treated like a king as he enters Yerushalem.


As they got close to Yerushalem and were passing Bethphage and Bethany on the Mt. of Olives, Yeshua sent two of his apprentices ahead, telling them, “Go on to the next village and as you enter it, you'll see a young donkey that hasn't ever been ridden yet. Untie it and bring it back here. If anyone asks what you're doing, tell them that the master needs it and he'll return it later.”

So they went ahead and found a donkey tied up on the path just outside the door of a house, and so they went to untie it. But some of the people standing around there said, “Hoy! What do you think you're doing untying that donkey?”

So they responded just as Yeshua had told them, and the people allowed them to proceed. They brought the young donkey to Yeshua, placing their coats over it before Yeshua mounted it. As Yeshua rode it into Yerushalem, many people laid their coats on the road, while others lay down leafy branches cut from trees in nearby fields. Some went ahead and others came along behind, shouting, “This is the saviour who comes with the authority and blessing from Yahweh— the promised ancestor of King David who is blessed as he ushers in his kingdom—the blessed saviour from heaven.”

And so Yeshua arrived in Yerushalem and went into the temple and looked all around, but because it was now evening, he went back out to Bethania village with his twelve apprentices.
