Right now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 4 35 to 41.
The section heading is: Yeshua halts the storm on the lake.


It was already becoming evening on that day, so Yeshua said to his apprentices, “Let's all go across to the other shore.” So they left the crowd behind and got into the boat with him. Out in the lake a big storm came up with strong winds, and the waves started breaking across the boat and filling it. But Yeshua slept on in the back, sleeping on a cushion. They woke him and cried, “Teacher, don't you care that we're all about to drown?”

Now awake, Yeshua scolded the wind and waves, “Quieten down! Stop that!” Then the wind died down and the water became very calm. And turning back to them he asked, “Why were you all so afraid? Don't you have any faith yet?”

But now they were very afraid again and asked each other, “Who could this man be that both the wind and waves obey him?”
