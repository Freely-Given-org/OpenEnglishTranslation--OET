Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 3 20 to 30.
The section heading is: The two opposing spiritual powers.


Then Yeshua was approaching a house, but the crowd swarmed them again so they had no opportunity to eat. The ones with him heard all the noise and came to get him, because they were wondering if he was crazy.

The religious teachers had come up from Yerushalem and were complaining, “He's possessed by the prince of demons. He can command them because he rules them!”

But Yeshua addressed them: “How could Satan command himself? If a kingdom is divided against itself, that kingdom could never prosper. If a household is divided against itself, that family will never prosper. So if Satan attacked himself and was split, he couldn't possibly be powerful, but his time would be over.

On the contrary, no one can enter a strong man's house to clean out his possessions unless he first ties up that strong man—after that he can work on the goods.

Honestly, I assure you that any sin of a person can be forgiven, no matter how much they curse God. But anyone who speaks against the Holy Spirit can never be forgiven, but is eternally responsible for their sin.” (He said this because they were saying that he was demon-possessed.)
