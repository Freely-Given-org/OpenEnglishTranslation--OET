Next we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 14 17 to 26.
The section heading is: Yeshua shocks his apprentices at the Passover meal.


As the evening approached, Yeshua went in for dinner with his twelve apprentices. As they were sitting around eating, he told them, “I'll tell you frankly that one of you eating with me here will hand me over to be arrested.” That upset them, and several of them individually asked, “Surely it couldn't be me?”

Then he continued, “It's one of you twelve—the one who dips his bread in the sauce at the same time as me. Indeed it's written in the scriptures that humanity's child will be arrested, but on the other hand, what a tragedy it'll be for the one who hands him over—in fact it would be better for that man if he was never born.”

As they carried on eating, he took some bread, blessed God, broke it apart and gave a piece to them, saying, “Take this bread—it's my body.”

Then he picked up a cup, gave thanks to God, and passed it around so they could each drink from it, saying, “This is the blood of my contract that will be poured out for many people. I can assure you that I won't drink anything made from grapes until I drink new juice in God's kingdom.”

And after they'd sung some songs, they left the town and went across to the Mount of Olives.
