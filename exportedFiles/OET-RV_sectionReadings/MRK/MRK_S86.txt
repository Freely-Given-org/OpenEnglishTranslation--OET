Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 15 16 to 20.
The section heading is: The battalion plays with their mock ‘king’.


So the soldiers led Yeshua inside the palace that was the governor's residence, and called in the rest of the battalion. They mocked Yeshua by dressing him in a kingly robe, and after someone twisted some thorns together into a mock crown, they pressed it onto his head, and began saluting him, “Greetings, king of the Jews.” They took turns whacking his head with a stick and spitting on him, while others knelt down in front of him and pretended to be bowing to him. After they'd finished having fun mocking him, they took the purple robe off him, and put his own robe back on him. Then they led him outside towards the execution spot.
