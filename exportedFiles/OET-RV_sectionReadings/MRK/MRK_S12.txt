Right now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 2 18 to 22.
The section heading is: The question of fasting.


Now the followers of Yohan-the-immerser and members of the Pharisees regularly fasted. So a small group came to Yeshua and asked, “How come Yohan's followers and the Pharisee party fast regularly, yet your followers don't?”

And Yeshua answered, “Can the friends of the groom fast when they're celebrating along with him? No, as long as he's still there, they won't be fasting. But in the future, the groom will be taken away from them, and then they'll be fasting.

“No one sews a patch of new, unshrunken cloth onto an old garment, because the new patch will tear away from that old fabric and it will end up as a worse tear. And no one puts new wine into old wineskins, because the wine will burst the already stretched wineskins, and then both the wine and the wineskins will be destroyed. New wine must be put into new wineskins.”
