Next we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 4 1 to 9.
The section heading is: The parable about planting seed.


Again Yeshua started teaching by the edge of the lake. A huge crowd gathered around him, but he had boarded a boat that was floating in the water, and all the people were there on the land. From there, he taught them many different things in parables, including this one: “Listen. Once there was a farmer who went to spread some seed. As he spread it, some landed on the path, and the birds came and fed on it. Some landed on a rocky part without a lot of soil, and it quickly sprouted in the warm, shallow soil, but when the sun came up, it dried out because the roots weren't deep, and so it just withered up. Some of the seed landed in a thistle patch, and the thistles grew and choked it out, so the plants never bore a crop. But some seed landed in fertile soil and it bore a harvest—some of it multiplying thirty times, some sixty, and some a hundred times.”

Then Yeshua added, “Anyone who wants to understand, let them consider it.”
