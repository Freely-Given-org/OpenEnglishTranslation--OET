Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 12 18 to 27.
The section heading is: Yeshua explains that those who’ve died are alive.


Another time, members of the Sadducee sect came to Yeshua and asked, “Teacher, Mosheh wrote that if a man's married brother should die before his wife without having any children, then his brother should marry her to produce descendants in his brother's name. Now what if there were seven brothers and the first one got married and died childless. So then the second brother took her in, but again died childless, and then the third. Eventually all seven brothers died childless, and finally the woman also died. Now, at the resurrection of the dead, which brother will she be the wife of, because at some point, she was married to each of the seven of them?”

So Yeshua answered, “Actually you're all quite wrong, because you ignore the scriptures and also because you haven't experienced God's power. When the dead do come back to life, they won't be getting engaged or married, but will be like the messengers in heaven. And as for the state of those who've died, didn't you read in the scriptures about Mosheh when God spoke to him in front of the burning thorn bush and said, ‘I am the god of Abraham and the god of Isaac and the god of Yacob’? So God is not the god of the dead, but of the living. You Sadducees are very wrong!”
