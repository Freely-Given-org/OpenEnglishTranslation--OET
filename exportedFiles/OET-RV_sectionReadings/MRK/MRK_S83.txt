Right now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 14 66 to 72.
The section heading is: Peter disowns Yeshua.


Meanwhile, Peter was down in the courtyard when one of the chief priest's servant girls came out, and seeing Peter warming himself, she looked more closely and said, “Hey, you were with that Yeshua from Nazareth!”

But he denied it, “I don't know him and I don't know why you think I would!” And he moved away from the fire.

But the girl that had seen him said to some of the others standing around, “That guy's one of them.”

But again Peter denied it.

A little while later, some of those standing there said, “You must be one of them because you're from Galilee.”

Now Peter started swearing and cursing and said, “I've never even met that fellow that you're all talking about!”

Right then the rooster crowed for the second time and Peter remembered now what Yeshua had told him—how he would renounce Yeshua three times before the rooster crowed—and he broke down and cried.
