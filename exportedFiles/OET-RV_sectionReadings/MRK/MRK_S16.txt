Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 3 13 to 19.
The section heading is: Yeshua appoints his twelve representatives.


As Yeshua started climbing up a hill, he called the followers that he had selected, and they went off with him. He appointed twelve of them as his representatives so that they could stick with him and be sent off to be proclaiming his message and to also have authority to exorcise demons.

These are the twelve that he appointed: Simon (but he appended the name “Peter”), Yacob and his brother Yohan who were Zebedee's sons (and he nicknamed them the “sons of thunder”), Andrew, Philip, Bartholomew, Matthew, Thomas, Yacob Alfaeus, Thaddeus, Simon from Canaan, and Yudas Iscariot who later betrayed him.
