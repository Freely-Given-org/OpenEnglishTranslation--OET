Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 3 1 to 6.
The section heading is: The man with the curled-up hand.


And again another time, Yeshua went to the meeting hall and there was a man there with a curled-up hand. The religious leaders there were watching him closely to see if he would heal the man on the rest day, and then if he did, they would have a case against him. Then Yeshua said to the man with the flawed hand, “Stand up there in the middle.” And he asked the leaders, “Is it allowable to do good on the rest day, or only to do evil? To save life, or to kill?” But they didn't reply.

After angrily looking around at them, grieving at their complete lack of compassion, he said to the man, “Open up your hand!” The man stretched out his fingers, and his hand became totally normal. But the Pharisees quickly left, and meeting with King Herod's supporters, they conspired to kill Yeshua.
