Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 10 35 to 45.
The section heading is: How to become respected in God’s kingdom.


Then Yacob and Yohan, the two sons of Zebedee approached Yeshua and asked, “Teacher, we'd like to ask a favour from you—please don't disappoint us.”

“What do you want from me?” Yeshua asked.

They answered, “Allow us two to sit beside you in heaven—one of us on each side of you.”

But Yeshua replied, “You don't even understand what you're asking for. Do you think you could endure the suffering that I have to go through? Or to be enveloped in the darkness that I have to go down into?”

And they replied, “Sure we can.”

“And indeed you all will suffer like me and be enveloped in darkness like I will be,” responded Yeshua, “but as for deciding who will sit next to me, that's not my decision to make because it's already been decided.”

When the ten other apprentices heard what these two had asked for, they were upset at Yacob and Yohan. But Yeshua called them over, “Listen, in most countries their rulers boss the people around and their leaders exert their authority over them. But it doesn't work like that with you—anyone of you who wanted to be honoured must serve the others, and anyone who wants to become the most respected has to work like a slave does. Even humanity's child didn't come to be served, but to serve others and to give his life as a ransom to set many people free.”
