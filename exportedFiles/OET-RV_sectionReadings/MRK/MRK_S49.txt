Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 9 42 to 50.
The section heading is: Difficult teachings about disobedience.


“But anyone who causes a young person to falter from their trust in me, it would be better if they were thrown into the sea with a heavy weight tied around their neck. If your hand causes you to disobey God, cut it off—it's better to enter eternal life being maimed, than to have two hands but to be thrown into the fire that never goes out. ◘ And if your foot causes you to disobey God, cut it off—it's better to enter eternal life being maimed, than to have two feet but to be thrown into hell. ◘ And if your eye causes you to disobey God, gouge it out—it's better to enter God's kingdom one-eyed, than to have two eyes and be thrown into hell where the worms never die and the fire never goes out.

“Everyone will be salted with fire.

“Now salt is good, but if it were to go bad, what would you season your food with then? So keep yourselves like salt, and strive to get on well with each other.”
