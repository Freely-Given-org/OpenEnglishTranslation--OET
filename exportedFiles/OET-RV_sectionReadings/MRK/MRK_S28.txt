Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 6 1 to 6.
The section heading is: Yeshua’s community can’t believe he’s from God.


So Yeshua left Capernaum and went back with his followers to Nazareth, his birthplace. It was now the Rest Day and when he started teaching in the meeting hall, many who heard him were astonished, saying, “Where did he learn all this? How could he have such wisdom and be able to do these miracles? Isn't this the carpenter, the son of Maria, and the brother of Yacob and Yosef and Yudas and Simon? Don't his sisters live around here?” And they were offended by his teaching.

Yeshua told them, “A prophet is only dishonoured in his own home town, and among his own relatives, and in his own home.”

And he wasn't able to do even one miracle there, except for placing his hands on a few sick people and healing them. He was amazed at how they wouldn't believe him as he went around the villages teaching.
