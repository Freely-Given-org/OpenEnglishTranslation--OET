Right now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 14 3 to 9.
The section heading is: The woman anoints Yeshua for his burial.


Meanwhile, Yeshua was in Bethany at the home of Simon who had previously had leprosy. Yeshua was sitting there when a woman came in with a hand-crafted container of very expensive fragrant oil. Breaking open the sealed flask, she poured the fragrant oil over Yeshua's head. Immediately some started quietly grumbling about the extravagant waste, then they scolded the woman because a flask of genuine lotion like this could easily fetch almost a year's wages if it was sold.

But Yeshua spoke up, “What do you think you're doing abusing her—leave her alone! She's done something very good for me. You'll always have poor people around, and you can help them whenever you want to, but I won't be around forever. She has performed the traditional anointing of my body in advance of my burial. And I can assure you that wherever the good news is proclaimed around the world, what she has done today will be part of the story and she'll be remembered for it.”
