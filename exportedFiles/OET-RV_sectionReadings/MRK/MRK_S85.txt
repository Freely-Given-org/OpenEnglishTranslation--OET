Today we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 15 6 to 15.
The section heading is: Pilate agrees to execute an innocent man.


Now at time of the Jewish Passover, Pilate would normally pardon one prisoner for them and release him. There was another prisoner at the time named Barabbas who was an activist accused of murder. By now, the crowd had arrived and asked Pilate to release a prisoner as usual at Passover, so he asked them, “Do you want me to release the King of the Jews for you?” (This was because Pilate was well aware that the chief priests had brought Yeshua because they were jealous of his popularity.) But the chief priests stirred up the crowd to start saying that it was Barabbas that they wanted released, so Pilate asked again, “Then what should I do with this man that you call the King of the Jews?”

The crowd yelled back, “Execute him on a pole.”

“What has he even done wrong?” Pilate demanded.

But they just yelled louder, “Execute him on a pole!”

Now Pilate wanted to placate the crowd, so he had Barabbas released while he had Yeshua flogged and then handed over to the execution squad.
