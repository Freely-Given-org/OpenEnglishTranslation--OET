Now we have a reading of a section from the Open English Translation of the bible, reading from the Readers Version Mark 4 13 to 20.
The section heading is: Explaining about the ground where the seed fell.


Then Yeshua said to them, “If you didn't understand this parable, how will you understand any others? The person spreading seed is spreading the good message, and there are some along the path where the good message is being spread, who hear, but Satan turns up immediately and snatches the message from them. Similarly, there's some seed that falls onto rocky places where the people who hear the good message happily accept it, but don't make strong roots—just temporary changes. So when they get mocked or attacked about accepting the good message, they quickly stumble. Others are the ones where the seed was spread among the thistles. They heard the good message, but contemporary topics, or the lure of money, or the appetite for other things, choke their good intentions and the message becomes unfruitful in them. But lastly, the seed that landed on the good soil represents the people who hear the good message, accept it, and are fruitful thirty, sixty, or even a hundred times over.”
